## What it is
Free webcam-based mouse-replacement / pointer software developed at Boston
College to help people with disabilities use a computer. Main audience: people
who do not have reliable control of a hand but who can move their head (e.g.
severe motor disability, cerebral palsy, ALS, traumatic brain injury).

## Origin
Invented ~1999-2000 by Prof. James Gips (Boston College) and Prof. Margrit
Betke (then BC, now Boston University). Descended from earlier work ("The Camera
Mouse: Visual Tracking of Body Features to Provide Computer Access for People
With Severe Disabilities," Betke, Gips & Fleming, IEEE Trans. Neural Systems and
Rehabilitation Engineering, 2002).

## How it works
The software uses a standard computer webcam to lock onto and track a chosen
small region of the user's face — e.g. a nostril or the tip of an eyebrow — and
maps that head/feature movement to the on-screen cursor position.

## Click mechanism (dwell)
Clicks are generated by dwell time: if the user keeps the pointer within a
region (typically ~30 pixel radius) for a set time (typically ~0.5 second), a
mouse click is generated. Radius and dwell time are configurable.

## Reach / downloads
More than 3,300,000 free downloads since the program was made freely available
in June 2007; roughly 2,500 downloads per month.

## Platform / license / cost
Free (no cost). Windows desktop application. Latest referenced user manuals:
Camera Mouse 2011 (v1.6) and 2013 (v2.1).
