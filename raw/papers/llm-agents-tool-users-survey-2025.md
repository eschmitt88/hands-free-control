# Survey: LLMs as Autonomous Agents and Tool Users

Title: "From Language to Action: A Review of Large Language Models as Autonomous
Agents and Tool Users"

Authors: Sadia Sultana Chowa, Riasad Alvi, Subhey Sadi Rahman, Md Abdur Rahman,
Mohaimenul Azam Khan Raiaan, Md Rafiqul Islam, Mukhtar Hussain, Sami Azam

Year: 2025 (submitted August 24, 2025; revised October 28, 2025)

Publication Status: Accepted for publication in Artificial Intelligence Review
(2026).

## Coverage Scope

The review examines papers published 2023-2025 from top-tier venues, investigating
how LLMs are used as decision-making agents for their ability to interpret
instructions, manage sequential tasks, and adapt through feedback.

## Key Topics Addressed

- Architectural design principles for LLM agents
- Single-agent vs. multi-agent systems deployment strategies
- External tool integration methodologies
- Cognitive mechanisms: reasoning, planning, and memory systems
- Prompting methods and fine-tuning effects on performance
- Benchmarks and assessment protocols with analysis of 68 public datasets

## Critical Findings

The authors identify verifiable reasoning capacities, self-improvement potential,
and personalization capabilities as central research areas, plus ten future
research directions to address existing gaps.
