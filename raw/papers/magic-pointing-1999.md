## Abstract-level content (search + domain knowledge)
Proposes MAGIC (Manual And Gaze Input Cascaded) pointing: eye gaze warps the
cursor to the vicinity of the gaze region, and the final fine selection is
completed by a conventional manual input actuator (mouse/trackpoint). The claim
is that this cascade offers reduced physical effort and fatigue vs manual
pointing, greater accuracy and naturalness than pure gaze pointing, and
potentially faster speed than manual pointing.

DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE (widely reported, not from a captured abstract): two variants
were proposed — a "liberal" approach (cursor proactively warps to wherever the
user looks, so the cursor is already near the likely target) and a
"conservative" approach (cursor warps only after manual input begins, avoiding
distracting cursor jumps). Evaluated with a Fitts'-law-style pointing task.
