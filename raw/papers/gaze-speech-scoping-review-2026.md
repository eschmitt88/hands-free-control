## Abstract-level content (paraphrased from search snippets)
Scoping review of 103 studies published between 1991 and 2025 that examine the
combined use of gaze and speech in HCI. Studies are organised into two
categories: explicit (users intentionally provide gaze and speech) and implicit
(systems leverage users' natural gaze/speech behaviours to support interaction).

Framing quote (search snippet): "Gaze and speech form a compelling pairing:
gaze provides rapid spatial grounding, while speech conveys rich semantic
information." Across both categories the authors identify recurring ways of
combining the two modalities to (a) resolve ambiguity, (b) ground references,
and (c) support adaptivity, while highlighting challenges of temporal
alignment, fusion, and privacy. The dominant explicit pattern is "gaze to
point, speech as command."
