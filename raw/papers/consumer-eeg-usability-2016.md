# Consumer-grade EEG devices: are they usable for control tasks?

**Authors:** Rytis Maskeliunas, Robertas Damasevicius, Ignas Martisius, Mindaugas Vasiljevas
**Institution:** Kaunas University of Technology (Multimedia Engineering, Software
Engineering, and Computer Science Departments), Lithuania
**Year:** 2016 · **Journal:** PeerJ · **DOI:** 10.7717/peerj.1746
**Access:** full text (PMC open access; key numbers captured).

## Abstract / captured content

The study evaluates two consumer-grade EEG devices (Emotiv EPOC and NeuroSky
MindWave) for concentration/relaxation and blinking recognition tasks with 10
subjects. Both devices showed high variability and non-normal data distribution,
making them difficult for control applications. The Emotiv EPOC demonstrated
superior performance across all tasks.

### Key numbers (captured)

- **Emotiv EPOC** — attention/meditation recognition accuracy ~60.5%; blinking
  recognition "more than 75%".
- **NeuroSky MindWave** — attention/meditation ~22.2% (near chance); blinking
  "less than 50%".
- **BCI illiteracy:** "up to 50% of users may be BCI-illicit [illiterate] when
  using low-cost EEG devices."
- Overall assessment: devices suitable only for "beginner level brain signal
  measurement and research"; "other means of command recognition ... are much
  more viable."
- **Multimodal recommendation:** combine EEG with other input modalities; a
  "gaze tracking device was faster and more accurate."
