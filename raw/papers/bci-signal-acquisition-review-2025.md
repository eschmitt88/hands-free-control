## Abstract / captured content (verbatim-leaning)

The paper examines direct communication between the human brain and external
devices through BCI technology. It categorizes signal acquisition into three
approaches: "non-implantation, intervention, and implantation techniques" while
analyzing classic paradigms, current classifications, and hybrid variants. The
authors emphasize understanding "the interdependence between paradigms and
signal acquisition technologies."

Core thesis: the review stresses "the synergy between paradigm design and signal
acquisition techniques" as essential for developing "more efficient,
user-friendly, and versatile BCI systems." It covers classic paradigms
(SSVEP, P300, motor imagery), current classifications, and hybrid paradigms.
