## Abstract (from arXiv preprint)
EyeTAP addresses the Midas touch problem in eye-tracking: distinguishing
intentional computer interactions from normal eye movement. EyeTAP combines
gaze tracking (for pointer position, captured by an eye tracker) with acoustic
input (an acoustic signal such as a tongue click, microphone tap, or verbal
command captured by a headset microphone) for hands-free, contact-free
point-and-select operations.

## Reported results (arXiv abstract + search)
- User study with 33 participants.
- Robust in the presence of ambient noise, with tolerance up to 70 dB.
- Faster movement time and faster task completion time, and lower cognitive
  workload, than voice recognition alone.
- Did NOT generally outperform the dwell-time method on time, but had a lower
  error rate than dwell-time in one of the experiments.
- Requires minimal interface design changes to integrate into existing systems;
  positioned as valuable for individuals with mobility limitations.
