# Gaze-supported Interaction
## Definition
Interaction in which gaze assists rather than fully drives input, offloading coarse pointing to the eyes while another modality handles precise or confirming actions.
## Why it matters here
It frames gaze's proper role in this project as a fast coarse pointer, not a full input device, sidestepping the accuracy and Midas-touch limits of gaze-only control.
