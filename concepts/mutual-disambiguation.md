# Mutual Disambiguation
## Definition
Using one modality to resolve ambiguity in another — gaze grounds ambiguous pronouns, while speech disambiguates spatial selections.
## Why it matters here
It is why gaze-plus-voice beats either alone: an underspecified spoken command becomes concrete once anchored to where the user looks, and a noisy gaze target becomes precise once the words name the action.
