# Gaze-point, Speech-command
## Definition
The dominant explicit multimodal pattern in which gaze supplies rapid spatial pointing and grounding while a spoken command supplies the action and its semantics.
## Why it matters here
This is the project's central thesis: gaze gives coarse "where" essentially for free, and voice carries the "what," together forming a hands-free control loop faster and less error-prone than either modality alone.
## Connections
- [[gaze-supported-interaction]] — this pattern is the flagship instance of gaze assisting rather than driving input
- [[gaze-suggests-x-confirms]] — the pattern operationalizes the suggest/confirm split, with speech as the confirm channel
- [[midas-touch-problem]] — pairing gaze with a spoken command is a direct remedy for unintended gaze activation
- [[dwell-free-selection]] — speech commands let selection fire without dwell timers
