# Human-centered Interface Design
## Definition
Designing control interfaces around user needs and preferences, especially in assistive and accessibility contexts.
## Why it matters here
It keeps the project grounded in what motor-impaired users actually want from voice and gaze control, rather than optimizing metrics that do not translate into real-world usability.
