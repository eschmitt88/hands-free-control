# Gaze and Speech in Multimodal Human-Computer Interaction: A Scoping Review

## TL;DR

Scoping review of 103 gaze-and-speech HCI studies (1991–2025) that organises the
literature into explicit vs implicit interaction and identifies "gaze to point,
speech as command" as the dominant explicit pattern — gaze gives rapid spatial
grounding, speech carries rich semantics.

## Claims

- Gaze + speech is a natural pairing: gaze = fast spatial grounding, speech =
  rich semantic content.
- The field splits into **explicit** (user intentionally supplies both signals)
  and **implicit** (system exploits natural gaze/speech behaviour).
- Recurring combination strategies serve three ends: resolving ambiguity,
  grounding references, and supporting adaptivity.
- Persistent open challenges: temporal alignment of the two streams, fusion, and
  privacy.

## Methods

- Scoping review methodology over 103 studies spanning 1991–2025.
- Studies categorised (explicit/implicit) and coded for interaction patterns and
  combination strategies. (Full task taxonomy is in the paper; only
  abstract-level detail was accessible to this ingest — see access note.)

## Results

- "Gaze to point, speech as command" identified as the dominant explicit
  pattern — directly the design template for hands-free desktop control.
- Maps how prior systems resolve ambiguity / ground deictic references, which
  frames the whole gaze+voice design space this project sits in.
