# A Review of Brain-Computer Interface Technologies: Signal Acquisition Methods and Interaction Paradigms

## TL;DR

A 2025 arXiv survey arguing that BCI paradigm design (SSVEP, P300, motor imagery,
hybrids) and signal-acquisition method (non-invasive / interventional / implanted)
are interdependent and must be co-designed. Framing-only for this project — no
per-paradigm bandwidth numbers were captured.

## Claims

- Signal acquisition splits into three families: non-implantation (e.g. EEG),
  intervention, and implantation.
- Paradigm choice and acquisition technology are interdependent; effective BCIs
  require co-design ("synergy between paradigm design and signal acquisition").
- Covers classic (SSVEP, P300, MI), current, and hybrid paradigms.

## Methods

- Narrative literature review (20 pages, 12 figures). No experiments.

## Results

- No quantitative results captured; the abstract page carried only framing. Any
  ITR/accuracy comparisons would require the full PDF.

## Critique / open questions

- Preprint, not peer-reviewed; institutions not listed on the abs page — hard to
  weight authority.
- For a *hands-free desktop-control* project this is orientation material only:
  it maps the paradigm landscape but does not tell us which (if any) EEG paradigm
  clears the bandwidth/reliability bar for real desktop control. EEG remains a
  likely-park modality.
- Would need the PDF to extract the comparative tables that justify the
  "interdependence" thesis.
