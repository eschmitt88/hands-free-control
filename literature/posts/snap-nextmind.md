## TL;DR
Snap's 2022 page announcing it acquired NextMind, a Paris BCI startup whose
~$399 dry-electrode headband decoded visual attention (VEP) for cursor/selection.
Post-acquisition the hardware and dev portal were shut down - a cautionary
data point on consumer-BCI product longevity and commercial viability.

## Key points
- NextMind: non-invasive, focus-driven "hands-free" interaction for computers and
  AR/VR; explicitly not thought-reading nor stimulating the brain.
- Mechanism (external knowledge, not stated verbatim on page): occipital VEP /
  visual-attention decoding via dry electrodes - a selection interface, not
  free-form control.
- The Snap page confirms the acquisition and Snap Lab AR research direction;
  it does NOT confirm the $399 price, dry-electrode spec, or shutdown - those are
  from the candidate brief / external context and are flagged unverified in raw.
- Commercial-viability signal: a working, shipping consumer BCI product was
  discontinued after an acqui-hire. Building on such platforms carries
  abandonment risk.

## Follow-up
- If VEP-selection is the target paradigm, prefer open/reproducible stacks
  (OpenBCI + SSVEP) over dependence on a discontinued vendor product.
- Verify the $399 price and shutdown timeline from an independent source before
  citing them (not confirmed by this page).
