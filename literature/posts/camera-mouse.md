# Camera Mouse — free webcam head-tracking pointer (Boston College)

## TL;DR
Camera Mouse is a free Windows program (Boston College, Gips & Betke, ~1999-2000)
that tracks a small chosen facial feature (e.g. a nostril or eyebrow tip) via a
standard webcam and maps head movement to the cursor, with dwell-time clicking.
It is aimed at people who cannot reliably control a hand but can move their head,
and has passed 3.3M+ free downloads since 2007.

## Key points
- Feature-tracking, not full head-pose: user picks a small face region and the
  software locks onto and tracks it, translating movement to cursor position.
- Dwell click: keeping the pointer within ~30 px for ~0.5 s (both configurable)
  fires a click — the canonical dwell-click design.
- Target users: severe motor disability (cerebral palsy, ALS, TBI, etc.).
- Reach: >3.3M downloads since June 2007, ~2,500/month — de facto reference in
  webcam head-pointing assistive tech.
- Backed by peer-reviewed research (Betke, Gips & Fleming, IEEE TNSRE 2002).
